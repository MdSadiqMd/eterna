### Hot path breakdown

| Rank | Function | Samples | % | Notes |
|------|----------|---------|---|-------|
| 1 | `shared::orderbook::OrderBook::submit` | 13 | 61.9% | Matching loop — expected dominant path |
| 2 | `alloc::alloc::dealloc` | 3 | 14.3% | `VecDeque` buffer freed when a price level drains |
| 3 | `alloc::raw_vec::RawVec::grow_one` | 2 | 9.5% | `VecDeque` realloc on first order at new level |
| 3 | `alloc::collections::vec_deque::VecDeque::grow` | 2 | 9.5% | Same realloc path |
| 4 | `BTreeMap::remove` | 2 | 9.5% | Empty level cleanup after full fill |
| 5 | `BTreeMap::entry::or_default` | 1 | 4.8% | New price level insertion |

### Key findings

**62% in `OrderBook::submit`** — the matching loop is the hot path, which is correct. BTreeMap iteration and VecDeque mutation dominate.

**~33% is heap churn** — every fully-consumed price level frees its `VecDeque` backing buffer; every new level allocates one. Under a high fill rate (aggressive orders sweeping multiple levels) this becomes the second-biggest cost.

**`BTreeMap::remove` is visible** — O(log n) per removal, called when a level hits zero qty. Fine at 20 levels, would grow with a wider price range.

**Nothing in serde, HTTP, or tokio** — the bench exercises core matching in isolation, confirming the allocator is the next bottleneck.

### Potential optimisations

1. **Pool `VecDeque` buffers** — instead of dropping the backing allocation when a level empties, keep a free-list and reuse buffers. Eliminates the dealloc → malloc round-trip on the hot path.
2. **Price ladder array** — replace `BTreeMap<u64, VecDeque<Order>>` with a fixed array indexed by tick offset. O(1) access vs O(log n), eliminates tree node allocations entirely. Only viable if the tick range is bounded.
3. **`VecDeque::with_capacity`** on first insert at a new level — avoids the `grow_one` realloc when the first order arrives at a previously-empty price.
